THE GIUGIARO WAY

ADI Compasso d'oro Museum posters

The project brief was to create a set of three posters using three designs from the ADI Compasso d'oro Museum as subjects. The chosen designs had to be selected based on a concept, such as products, spaces, or graphics by a particular designer, in order to celebrate their work. Ultimately, the posters needed to tell a story.

Giorgetto Giugiaro, born in Garessio, Italy in 1938, is widely recognized as the world’s most influential modern automotive designer.Throughout his career, he has designed over 200 cars. In 1999, a jury of more than 120 journalists from around the world named Giugiaro the “Designer of the Century.” My concept for the posters revolved around celebrating Giugiaro’s impressive trajectory over the years, which I named “The Giugiaro Way.”

The Fiat Panda, winner of the 1981 Compasso d’Oro Award, achieved a perfect balance by breaking away from the past perception that utilitarian cars were small and uncomfortable. It managed to maintain a utilitarian appearance while offering greater interior volume, affordability, and reliability.

The Fiat Punto, winner of the 1994 Compasso d’Oro Award, demonstrated the manufacturer’s daring approach to overcome the crisis. The car’s design suggests the idea of a single metal body formed through casting, seamlessly integrating other components and materials.

The Alfa Romeo Brera, winner of the 2004 Compasso d’Oro Award, showcased a rare and complex combination of volumes in its body design, expressed through clear-cut, essential, and harmonious lines. The result was one of the most beautiful and captivating cars ever produced.
