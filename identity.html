PERSONAL IDENTITY

Myself through design

Creating one's identity can be an incredibly challenging task, especially for designers. Despite possessing a keen eye for aesthetics and a deep understandind of effective design principles, we often struggle when it comes to designing for ourselves. Few challenges can be as frustrating as attempting to define and craft our own identity.

To tackle this challenge, I approached it as if I were working for a client. I wrote a comprehensive brief with specific objectives and identified a set of tools that would effectively promote both myself and my work. What sets me apart and makes me suitable for any task, project, or job is my unique method, which encompasses my working style, skills, personality, taste, interests, and experience.

Right from the beginning, I knew that my identity should be treated like any other design I have created, maintaining consistency across all applications. Elements such as typography, shapes, and colors played a crucial role in representing my concept, method, and myself.

The initial step involved designing the logo, which served as the foundation for defining a cohesive system and selecting the tools through which my identity would be presented and communicated.

This project holds great significance to me as it reflects how I perceive myself through design and how I want you, the reader, to perceive my personal identity.
